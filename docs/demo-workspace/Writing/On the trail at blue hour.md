# On the trail at blue hour

By the time the light turns fully blue, Sly stops trotting ahead and starts checking back every few steps, as if he has decided that the path belongs to both of us now.

The fir trees go flat and dark. The gravel softens underfoot. Every sound carries a little farther than it should.

He pauses at the edge of the field, ears up, listening hard to something I cannot hear. Then the moment passes, and he turns back with that bright, practical expression that seems to say: all right, keep moving.
